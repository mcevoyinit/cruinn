The problem

A recording is not a usable record.

An interview is captured, and there the recording stops. Turning it into a record that holds up to disclosure and to a court is a separate problem, and for most interviews it goes unsolved.

Recorded by law

Criminal Justice Act 1984 regulations, SI 74/1997

Custody interviews have been recorded for decades. Turning those recordings into a written record a case can rely on is still the exception, not the rule.

Hundreds of euro

per interview, and days of waiting

Commissioning a transcript of a single interview is expensive and slow. Cost and delay are why so many recordings are never transcribed at all.

No proof it is unaltered

in an ordinary transcript

A plain document shows the words but not their integrity. Nothing in it demonstrates that a line was not quietly changed after the fact.

How it works

The evidence lifecycle

Every transcript follows one controlled path, from the moment a recording is received to a sealed record anyone can verify. Machine speed where it helps, trained human judgement where it matters, and a cryptographic seal that makes any later change evident.

  1. 1

    Intake and chain of custody

    Received over a secure channel and hashed at ingest, opening an auditable chain of custody.

  2. 2

    AI draft

    A sovereign model on our own infrastructure produces the first pass, tuned for Irish accents.

  3. 3

    Human certifies

    A vetted reviewer corrects and certifies every line against the audio.

  4. 4

    Cryptographic seal

    An append only log and a Merkle root seal the record, so any later change is detectable.

  5. 5

    Disclosure copies

    Certified copies are issued for disclosure and for court, each carrying the seal.

  6. 6

    Verifiable forever

    Anyone can verify the seal independently and offline, without Cruinn.

Trust and sovereignty

Records you can stand over

A record used in a formal setting has to be defensible. In Cruinn, integrity, provenance and sovereignty are properties of the system, not promises on a page.

Data stays in Ireland

Recordings and transcripts are processed and stored within Ireland. Sensitive material stays under Irish jurisdiction throughout.

No external AI services

Transcription runs on our own models, on infrastructure we control. No interview audio is sent to an external AI service or a third party cloud.

Append only audit trail

Every step, from receipt to delivery, is written to an append only log. Nothing can be quietly changed or removed.

Tamper evident seal

Each certified transcript carries a cryptographic seal. Alter a single character and the seal no longer verifies, so any change is plain to anyone who checks.

Independent offline verification

The seal can be checked by anyone, offline, without trusting Cruinn and without contacting us. Verification does not depend on us staying in business.

Vetted reviewers

Reviewers are trained and security vetted for sensitive material and work under strict confidentiality.

Security and assurance

We state our assurance status plainly and do not claim what we do not hold. The service is designed to align with GDPR and the EU Law Enforcement Directive, with data minimisation and access controls built in. Formal certifications are in progress as the service matures.

  • In progress ISO 27001 information security management
  • Planned SOC 2 reporting

Who it serves

Made for every side of the record

Investigators, prosecutors, defence and the court are not asking for different transcripts. They are asking for the same one, and for confidence that it is unchanged no matter whose hands it is in.

For investigations

An interview becomes a record ready for disclosure, with the chain of custody intact. Every transcript is hashed at intake and sealed on delivery, so the record that leaves an investigation is provably the record that was made.

For prosecution and defence

Both sides verify the same sealed record independently and offline, without relying on the other's copy or on ours. Agreement can start from a shared, checkable fact rather than a document taken on trust.

For courts and tribunals

A certified transcript whose integrity does not rest on anyone's word. The seal can be checked in the room, and any alteration to a sealed record is evident to whoever checks it.

Common questions

Questions, answered plainly

Where is the data processed and stored?

Within Ireland, always. Recordings and transcripts are processed and stored on infrastructure under Irish jurisdiction. No interview audio is sent to an external AI service or a third party cloud.

Does AI decide what the record says?

No. A sovereign model on our own infrastructure produces a fast first draft, but it certifies nothing. A vetted reviewer checks and certifies every line against the audio, so the certified text is the reviewer's judgement, not the machine's.

What happens if a transcript is altered later?

The seal stops verifying. Alter a single character of a sealed transcript and its cryptographic seal no longer matches, so the change is evident to anyone who checks, whoever made it.

Can the seal be verified without Cruinn?

Yes, independently and offline. Verification checks the certified text against its sealed manifest using published methods, so it does not depend on contacting us or on us remaining in business.

How does this fit disclosure obligations?

Certified copies are issued for disclosure and for court, each carrying the seal, and any party that receives one can check it. Cruinn supplies the certified transcript and its proof; how it is used in a given case remains a matter for those conducting it.
